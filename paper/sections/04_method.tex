\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

Section~\ref{sec:setup-channels} identified two objective readouts and their
candidate-side exposure signals. We now give the paper's central estimator:
random loss-shake energy recovers candidate gradient energy without a
candidate backward pass. We then specify hidden-state proximity, the fixed
endpoint router, and guarded repair. A continuous channel mixture appears
only as a secondary diagnostic; it is not a routing rule or a second
methodological contribution.

\subsection{Gradient-Channel Probe: Randomized Sensitivity}
\label{sec:method-gradient}

Let $B$ be a declared $d_B$-dimensional parameter block,
$\ell_x(\theta)=\ell(x;\theta)$, and draw shared seeded directions
$v_r\sim\operatorname{Unif}(\mathbb S^{d_B-1})$. Write
$\ell^\pm_{x,r}=\ell_x(\theta_0\pm\eta v_r)$. One identity is the
mathematical core of the method:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
    \widehat q_G(x)
    &=
    \frac{d_B}{R}\sum_{r=1}^{R}
    \left(\frac{\ell^+_{x,r}-\ell^-_{x,r}}{2\eta}\right)^2,\\[-1mm]
    \mathbb E_v[\widehat q_G(x)]
    &=
    \lVert\nabla_B\ell_x(\theta_0)\rVert_2^2+O(\eta^2).
\end{aligned}
}
\label{eq:loss-shake-identity}
\end{equation}
The central difference observes how much a candidate's loss shakes along a
random parameter direction; squaring removes its sign, and averaging over
isotropic directions converts response energy into gradient energy. Thus
we never try to infer the unlearner's update direction. We use
\emph{loss-shake energy} and \emph{randomized sensitivity} interchangeably
for $\widehat q_G$. The known factor
$d_B$ and the square root are rank-invariant and may be omitted in code.
The exact gradient norm remains an expensive reference, requiring one
candidate backward evaluation. In contrast, shared directions profile the
entire candidate universe in $2R$ batched forward sweeps, at inference
memory, without materializing a per-example gradient. Algorithm~\ref{alg:profile} states the resulting backward-free profiler.

\paragraph{Fidelity gate.}
The identity is asymptotic in $\eta$, so the estimator is used only after a
pre-outcome gate verifies that the perturbation is numerically realized and
that finite-difference rankings agree with exact projections on a diagnostic
subset. This separates underflow or cancellation at small radii from
higher-order contamination at large radii. The complete perturbation report,
finite-dimensional Monte Carlo variance, and $A/B/C$ decomposition are in
Appendix~\ref{app:fidelity-gate}; $\eta$ and $R$ are frozen before target
trajectories are unsealed.

\paragraph{Direction is a baseline, not our target.}
For comparison, one can replace the random directions by the normalized
initial forget gradient and estimate the signed alignment
$\langle\nabla_B\ell_x,\hat g_f\rangle$. This single-direction hypothesis
is reported as a prespecified cautionary baseline. Its preregistered
failure mode is rotation: the score is anchored to the initial forget
gradient, from which a multi-step trajectory rotates away, whereas the
isotropic energy average is invariant to that rotation. Its numerical
validation and trajectory analysis are in
Appendix~\ref{app:alignment-impl}.

\subsection{Representation-Channel Probe: Hidden-State Proximity}
\label{sec:method-representation}

The representation channel damages candidates whose hidden states occupy
the displaced region, so its matched probe is proximity, not gradient
magnitude. Candidate and forget examples are embedded with mean-pooled
final hidden states of the pre-unlearning model; each candidate is scored
by $\widehat q_H(x)$, its mean cosine similarity to its $k$ nearest forget
examples. The
score is forward-only and needs no parameter perturbation. Its overlap with
the gradient-energy profile is measured rather than assumed. External
sentence embeddings and lexical overlap provide additional members of the
same family and are reported as comparators.

\subsection{Channel Routing and Profile Construction}
\label{sec:method-routing}

Given a deletion request and a declared parent objective, the router reads
the objective's channel from its loss definition
(Section~\ref{sec:setup-channels}) and selects the matched probe family:
loss-shake gradient energy for output-channel parents, hidden-state
proximity for representation-channel parents. Equivalently, the
confirmatory rule is
$\alpha_{\mathrm{fixed}}(\mathcal U)=
\mathbb 1[c(\mathcal U)=\mathrm{representation}]$: output maps to endpoint $0$
and representation to endpoint $1$. Nothing in the router depends on candidate
damage or downstream outcomes; it is an objective-level lookup on the
frozen channel table.

The selected scorer assigns one value to every candidate in the declared
universe. For a fixed budget $K_p$, the protect pool is
$\mathcal P_{K_p}(r)=\operatorname{TopK}_{x\in\mathcal C_{\mathrm{disc}}}\,r(x)$,
with deterministic tie-breaking; a size-matched low-score pool
$\mathcal R_0(r)$ is sampled from a frozen near-zero quantile of the
discovery fold and serves as the repair-time reference stream. Pool size,
quantile, group-disjoint folds, seed, and fallback rules are fixed before
target outcomes are observed, and no audit candidate can enter either
pool.

\subsection{Secondary Diagnostic: Continuous Channel Mixture}
\label{sec:method-mixture}

The endpoint router above is the method. To diagnose cross-channel signal
without changing it, we place the two headline profiles on a common rank
scale. Let $\widetilde q_G$ and $\widetilde q_H$ be candidate-ID-tie-broken
rank transforms of $\widehat q_G$ and $\widehat q_H$ onto $[0,1]$ and
define
\begin{equation}
    s_\alpha(x)
    =
    (1-\alpha)\,\widetilde q_G(x)
    +
    \alpha\,\widetilde q_H(x),
    \qquad
    \alpha\in[0,1].
    \label{eq:mixture}
\end{equation}
Rank normalization is fixed because the two scores have different units; a
z-score variant is appendix-only. Under the deterministic tie rule,
$\alpha=0$ recovers the loss-shake allocation and $\alpha=1$ recovers the
hidden-state allocation, exactly so whenever the raw scores are tie-free.

\paragraph{Diagnostic discipline.}
Interior $\alpha$ values are never deployed, tuned, or used to revise the
declared channel. This exploratory extension was appended after the primary
design, then frozen before any mixture curve was computed on sealed
outcomes. Prediction and
protection curves show whether the two signals combine or interfere; their
argmax values are descriptive summaries observed after outcomes, not router
choices. The primary interaction is unchanged.
Section~\ref{sec:exp-alpha} states the frozen grids, and
Appendix~\ref{app:alpha-diagnostic} reports the full curves.

\subsection{Guarded Channel-Matched Repair}
\label{sec:method-protection}

The budget is spent by an engine-then-repair pipeline that leaves the
parent unlearner untouched. The parent runs from $\theta_0$ until its
first saved checkpoint satisfying the common forget criterion; a parent
that never reaches remains a reach failure and is not repaired. At the
reaching state we cache per-sequence and per-token forget losses as paired
references, then minimize answer-token NLL on $\mathcal P_{K_p}$.

Repair must not silently undo forgetting. At fixed refresh intervals we
compute mean forget and reference-stream gradients and project the repair
update away from their span, making those mean losses locally invariant to
first order up to the ridge regularization of the projection
(Appendix~\ref{app:guard-proof}). An identity-paired one-sided guard also penalizes sequence- or
token-level forgetting regression beyond a fixed tolerance. At audited
refreshes, a violating step is rejected, the last accepted parameters are
restored, and the step size is reduced. The exact guard and its tail bound
are given in Appendix~\ref{app:guard-proof}. The output is the final saved
checkpoint that still satisfies the forget criterion; retention is then evaluated only on the sealed audit. Algorithm~\ref{alg:repair} summarizes this engine-then-repair pipeline.

In the crossed design of Section~\ref{sec:setup-protection}, every
selector arm -- matched, mismatched, random -- runs this identical
pipeline with the identical budget. The frozen score-specific construction
changes the protect pool and its matched low-score reference stream together;
the crossed comparison therefore evaluates the complete allocation policy,
not a candidate-level causal effect of swapping only Top-$K_p$ membership.

\subsection{Scope of the Method}
\label{sec:method-scope}

The profile is a prospective risk measure, not a deletion certificate:
forgetting efficacy must still be established by direct, paraphrase, and
adversarial audits. The channel table covers where current objectives read
the model; an objective reading a third quantity would require a third
probe family, and hybrid objectives inherit partial signal from both. The
backward-free claim is scoped to candidate-side profiling: parents and
repair still backpropagate, as they must.
