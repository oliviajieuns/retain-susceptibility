\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

Given the forgetting-conditional target in Section~\ref{sec:setup}, we
construct a pre-update ordering over retained behaviors. It combines
direction-agnostic local loss susceptibility with request-conditioned
exposure, then uses the same sealed signal family for two distinct estimands:
prospective damage ranking and fixed-budget retain-side protection. All
scores and repair candidates are fixed at $\theta_0$ and hidden from the
parent unlearner.

\subsection{Local Susceptibility from Randomized Loss Perturbations}
\label{sec:method-gradient}
\label{sec:method-profile}

The realized update direction is unavailable at profiling time. To obtain an
objective-independent ordering reusable across parents, we measure how
strongly a retained loss could respond to any small perturbation in the
declared block $\mathcal B$. Under a fixed parameterization and norm,
first-order expansion gives
\begin{equation}
  \sup_{\substack{\operatorname{supp}(\Delta)\subseteq\mathcal B\\
                  \|\Delta\|_2\leq\epsilon}}
  \frac{|\ell_x(\theta_0+\Delta)-\ell_x(\theta_0)|}{\epsilon}
  =
  \|\nabla_{\mathcal B}\ell_x(\theta_0)\|_2+O(\epsilon).
  \label{eq:local-worst-direction}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:local-worst-direction} identifies
$q_G^\star(x)=\|\nabla_{\mathcal B}\ell_x(\theta_0)\|_2^2$ as a
direction-agnostic susceptibility prior: it measures potential local
response, not the sign or certainty of realized damage. Our exact reference
implementation uses candidate-side reverse mode. Loss-shake instead estimates
the same quantity from symmetric weight perturbations reused across all
candidates, making the signal available through forward loss evaluations. It
is an estimator of local sensitivity, not a new unlearning objective.

Draw $g_r\sim\mathcal N(0,I)$, set $v_r=g_r/\|g_r\|_2$, and embed $v_r$ as
zero outside the declared block $\mathcal B$ of dimension $d_{\mathcal B}$.
For radius $\eta$ and $R$ directions, define
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  \delta_r(x)
  &=
  \frac{\ell_x(\theta_0+\eta v_r)-\ell_x(\theta_0-\eta v_r)}
       {2\eta},\\
  \widehat q_G(x)
  &=
  \frac{d_{\mathcal B}}{R}\sum_{r=1}^{R}\delta_r(x)^2,
  \qquad
  \mathbb E_{\{v_r\}}[\widehat q_G(x)]
  =q_G^\star(x)+O(\eta^2).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:loss-shake-identity}
\end{equation}
The identity uses the symmetric directional derivative and
$\mathbb E[v_rv_r^\top]=I/d_{\mathcal B}$; bounded local third directional
derivatives give the $O(\eta^2)$ bias, while finite $R$ controls estimator
variance. The result depends on the parameterization, norm, and block
$\mathcal B$; we therefore use it for within-setting ranks, not absolute
cross-model comparison. When parent trainable supports differ, the manifest
records the common block and the score is interpreted only as a
restricted-block proxy.

Reusing each direction across the pool requires $2R$ forward sweeps from the
same restored parameter copy; paired losses are accumulated in fp32, with no
per-candidate gradient materialization. This gives a memory and access-pattern
advantage, not a universal FLOP or wall-time claim. Target-free data fix
$(R,\eta)$ using exact-energy rank agreement,
Top-$K_p$ overlap, perturbation survival, and direction-split stability; a
target run may pass only the corresponding integrity check and cannot retune
the estimator after damage is observed.

\subsection{Request-Conditioned Prospective Risk}
\label{sec:method-representation}
\label{sec:method-mixture}
\label{sec:method-channels}

Loss susceptibility alone cannot identify which deletion request will expose
it. Let $h(x;\theta_0)$ be the mean final hidden state over answer tokens and
let $\mathcal N_k(x;\mathcal D_f)$ be the $k$ forget examples with highest
cosine similarity to $x$. We define
\begin{equation}
  \widehat q_H(x)
  =
  \frac{1}{k}\sum_{z\in\mathcal N_k(x;\mathcal D_f)}
  \operatorname{cos}\!\left(h(x;\theta_0),h(z;\theta_0)\right).
  \label{eq:hidden-proximity}
\end{equation}
This forward-only term captures request-conditioned exposure, but remains a
proximity cue rather than evidence of causal dependence. Empirical midrank
maps fitted on $\mathcal C_{\mathrm{disc}}$ put the two signals on a common
scale, producing $\widetilde q_G,\widetilde q_H\in[0,1]$. Their convex rank
aggregation is
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  S_\alpha(x)
  &=(1-\alpha)\widetilde q_G(x)+\alpha\widetilde q_H(x),\\
  \pi_{\mathrm{pred}}
  &=\operatorname{Rank}_{x\in\mathcal C_{\mathrm{audit}}}
    S_{\widehat\alpha_{\mathrm{pred}}}(x),\\
  \mathcal P_{K_p}
  &=\operatorname{TopK}_{x\in\mathcal C_{\mathrm{disc}}}
    S_{\widehat\alpha_{\mathrm{prot}}}(x).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:mixture}
\end{equation}
The score is an ordering rule, not a calibrated probability or a causal
factorization. Prediction and protection use different weights because
ranking future damage and choosing examples that transfer under repair are
different decision problems. Disjoint target-free development folds select
the former by equal-request ranking correlation and the latter by
worst-request repair CVaR among feasible arms. At the target,
$\pi_{\mathrm{pred}}$ and $\mathcal P_{K_p}$ are sealed before unlearning;
audit candidates never enter repair, and audit outcomes choose neither
weight.

\subsection{Forgetting-Conditional Prediction}

The parent $u$ is a fixed external update operator rather than a component of
our predictor. It starts from $\theta_0$, follows its frozen objective,
trainable support, checkpoint grid, and budget, and receives no profile
information. Equation~\eqref{eq:forgetting-gate} selects its first checkpoint
satisfying the common primary direct-forgetting condition; lower
parent-objective loss alone is insufficient, and a non-reaching run produces
no conditional prediction or repair outcome.

Only after this condition is met do we open $d_{t^\dagger}$ on
$\mathcal C_{\mathrm{audit}}$ and compare it with the sealed ranking
$\pi_{\mathrm{pred}}$. The target is therefore retained damage
\emph{given the declared forgetting condition}, not whether the parent's
optimization loss decreases. This makes the test prospective: realized
damage cannot revise the score, entry checkpoint, or any target choice.

\subsection{Constrained Protection as Risk Allocation}
\label{sec:method-protection}

Protection casts the sealed ordering as a constrained allocation problem:
under a fixed retain-side budget, which discovery behaviors should receive
repair capacity after $\theta_{t^\dagger}$? Any benefit must transfer to
repair-held-out audit behaviors and is not implied by predictive correlation.
All arms start from the same entry checkpoint. For the sealed discovery pool
$\mathcal P_{K_p}$, we reduce retained loss while a selector-independent
neutral stream $\mathcal R_0$ anchors the entry model:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  L_{\mathcal P}(\theta)
  &=\frac{1}{K_p}\sum_{x\in\mathcal P_{K_p}}\ell_x(\theta),\\
  L_0(\theta)
  &=\frac{1}{|\mathcal R_0|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal R_0}
    \operatorname{KL}\!\left(p_{t^\dagger}(\cdot|x)\,\|\,p_\theta(\cdot|x)\right),\\
  L_{\mathrm{rep}}(\theta)&=L_{\mathcal P}(\theta)+\beta L_0(\theta).
\end{aligned}
  \label{eq:budgeted-repair}
\end{equation}
We seek to lower $L_{\mathrm{rep}}$ but return only checkpoints satisfying
$\theta\in\mathcal F$ and $N_{\mathrm{tok}}\le B_{\mathrm{tok}}$.
Here $\mathcal F$ is the predeclared forgetting/utility region, and
$B_{\mathrm{tok}}$ charges all repair-time model tokens. For differentiable
guard surrogates $c_j(\theta)\leq0$, let
$\mathcal A=\{j:c_j(\theta)\geq-\kappa_j\}$ and
$G=[\nabla c_j(\theta)]_{j\in\mathcal A}$. We use the damped
constraint-gradient filter
\begin{equation}
  \bar g_{\mathrm{rep}}
  =
  \left[I-G(G^\top G+\lambda I)^{-1}G^\top\right]
  \nabla L_{\mathrm{rep}}(\theta).
  \label{eq:repair-projection}
\end{equation}
A tentative update along $-\bar g_{\mathrm{rep}}$ is accepted only when
anchored forget and utility guards pass at token and example level; otherwise
it is rolled back and the step size is halved. These checks are necessary
because $\mathcal F$ also includes non-differentiable generation metrics.
Refresh, retry, and tolerance rules are frozen on development data
(Appendix~\ref{app:repair-protocol}).

All active arms share the entry checkpoint, neutral stream, order, seed,
token budget, guards, and update rule; only the selected discovery pool
changes. Selection returns the last saved checkpoint in $\mathcal F$ without
consulting retained-audit outcomes, and an arm with no feasible checkpoint is
reported as infeasible. The unchanged $\theta_{t^\dagger}$ is the
no-protection baseline. This comparison therefore isolates risk allocation
rather than introducing another unlearning objective. Prediction and
protection remain separate tests of the same pre-update profile: predictive
association neither guarantees repair effectiveness nor constitutes a
deletion certificate.
