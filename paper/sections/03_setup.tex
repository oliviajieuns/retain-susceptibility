\section{Problem Setup: From Profiling to Prediction and Protection}
\label{sec:setup}

Our question is not whether a parent objective such as NPO can optimize its
training loss. It is which retained behaviors are harmed once that parent
meets a predeclared direct-forgetting condition, and whether pre-target risk
can guide a limited repair budget. Figure~\ref{fig:method-overview} makes the conditional
order explicit: profile and seal at the frozen model, run an unchanged parent,
apply a common forgetting gate, and only then evaluate prediction or perform
guarded repair.

\subsection{Forgetting as an External Condition}
\label{sec:setup-prediction}

Let $\theta_0$ be the model before unlearning, let $\mathcal D_f$ be the
deletion request, and let $\mathcal C$ be an enumerated universe of retained
prompt--answer behaviors. The candidate universe need not be the retain stream
$\mathcal D_r$ used by a parent unlearner.

A parent $u$ produces saved checkpoints $\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_T$ by
optimizing its own objective $\mathcal J_u$. A decrease in $\mathcal J_u$ is
optimization evidence, not our definition of successful forgetting and not
our prediction target. Instead, metrics and thresholds external to
$\mathcal J_u$ define a common gate. Its entry time is
\begin{equation}
    t^\dagger
    =\min\left\{t\in\mathcal T_{\mathrm{save}}:
      \mathsf{ForgetOK}(\theta_t;\mathcal D_f)=1\right\},
    \label{eq:forgetting-gate}
\end{equation}
where the gate and saved-checkpoint set are fixed before the target run. A
trajectory with no such checkpoint is recorded as non-reaching.
The entry gate uses the common primary direct-forgetting criterion. After
repair, a broader final contract additionally checks paraphrase forgetting,
extraction and generation limits, and a general-utility floor.

For retained behavior $x$, let $\ell(x;\theta)$ be mean answer-token negative
log-likelihood with prompt tokens masked. Its realized damage is
\begin{equation}
    d_t(x)=\ell(x;\theta_t)-\ell(x;\theta_0).
    \label{eq:realized-damage}
\end{equation}
Positive values mean that the retained answer became less likely. The primary
prediction target is $d_{t^\dagger}(x)$: forgetting is the condition for a
comparable run, whereas retained damage is the outcome to predict. This
outcome is observed only after the trajectory and is never an input to target
profiling, pool selection, or checkpoint selection.

\subsection{Why a Pre-Target Two-Signal Profile?}

A prospective profile must distinguish two quantities: how strongly a
retained loss could respond to an unknown local update, and how strongly that
behavior is exposed to the current deletion request. Over the declared block
$\mathcal B$, the ideal susceptibility is
$q_G^\star(x)=\|\nabla_{\mathcal B}\ell_x(\theta_0)\|_2^2$. It supplies a
pre-update sensitivity prior rather than a prediction of signed damage.
Loss-shake energy $\widehat q_G$ estimates this quantity by reusing shared
weight perturbations across the candidate pool, while hidden-state proximity
$\widehat q_H$ supplies request-specific exposure. A behavior may be locally
sensitive but unrelated to the request, or request-proximal but locally
stable; the two signals therefore answer complementary questions rather than
forming a causal decomposition.

This separation yields three claim-bearing questions.

\paragraph{RQ1---Prospective risk.}
Conditional on the common forgetting gate, can a profile sealed at $\theta_0$
identify candidate-level retained damage and capture its harmful tail on
repair-held-out audit behaviors better than either component alone?

\paragraph{RQ2---Loss-shake fidelity and added value.}
Does loss-shake approximate the exact gradient-energy ranking at $\theta_0$
without candidate-side backpropagation, and does it add prospective
information beyond proximity and simple controls? Fidelity alone is
insufficient if gradient sensitivity does not improve damage prediction.

\paragraph{RQ3---Decision value.}
Can the frozen profile allocate a fixed repair budget that lowers sealed-audit
mean and worst-tail damage while keeping forgetting and utility within the
predeclared contract?

\subsection{Sealed Identification and Decision Contract}
\label{sec:setup-protection}

We split candidates by semantic group into discovery and audit folds,
$\mathcal C=\mathcal C_{\mathrm{disc}}\,\dot\cup\,
\mathcal C_{\mathrm{audit}}$. Target-free development requests separately fix
the numerical probe, prediction weight, protection weight, repair budget,
eligibility rules, and stopping rules. Three constraints identify the claims.

\paragraph{Pre-outcome sealing.}
At $\theta_0$, only $\mathcal C_{\mathrm{disc}}$ fits target rank maps or
supplies repair examples. The audit ranking and Top-$K_p$ eligible discovery
pool are frozen before unlearning; the parent receives neither, and realized
damage cannot revise them.

\paragraph{Conditional comparability.}
The unchanged parent runs from $\theta_0$, and only the external rule in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:forgetting-gate} determines entry at
$\theta_{t^\dagger}$. The frozen prediction ranking is then compared with
$d_{t^\dagger}$ on $\mathcal C_{\mathrm{audit}}$. This holds the forgetting
condition fixed rather than treating a decrease in parent-objective loss as
the outcome.

\paragraph{Common-operator evaluation.}
Every protection arm starts from the same $\theta_{t^\dagger}$ and receives
the same repair operator, reference stream, optimizer seed, and compute
budget; only its discovery selector changes. A final model must remain inside
the full forgetting/utility region before sealed-audit mean and tail damage
are compared. The unrepaired entry checkpoint is the no-protection baseline.

The prediction and protection weights are frozen on different development
folds because damage ranking and repair transfer are different estimands.
Accordingly, the framework tests two uses of one pre-update signal family; it
does not claim that prediction correlation itself causes successful repair.
Likewise, the final contract requires shared thresholds, not numerically
identical forgetting scores.
