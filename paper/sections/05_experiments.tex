\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

The experiments instantiate the two preregistered interaction endpoints of
Section~\ref{sec:setup} and the fidelity claims of
Section~\ref{sec:method}. All endpoints, channel assignments, probe
settings ($\eta$, $R$), and the dataset roster were frozen before the
confirmatory runs were unsealed. The evaluation addresses four questions:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Channel-conditioned prediction:} Across objectives from
    both channels, does the winning predictor family flip
    (interaction $\Delta>0$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:interaction})?

    \item \textbf{Backward-free fidelity:} How much of the exact
    gradient-norm profile does randomized sensitivity recover, at what
    cost, and where does the fidelity gate draw its validity boundary?

    \item \textbf{Channel-matched protection:} In the crossed design
    (parents $\times$ selectors), does the matched selector minimize
    sealed-audit tail damage per parent, at matched forgetting?

    \item \textbf{Robustness:} Does the interaction survive datasets with
    different forget--retain structure, seeds, requests, and the declared
    honesty clauses?
\end{enumerate}

Figure~\ref{fig:exp-anatomy} fixes the logic that every table below
shares. All experiments branch from one frozen starting point (the SFT'd
model, forget set, and candidate universe), and every predictor scores
every candidate exactly once at that point, before any unlearner is
chosen; those rankings are then sealed and never revised. Running one
unlearning objective creates one counterfactual world, and the ground
truth in that world is descriptive, not normative: it is the victim
distribution that this particular imperfect tool realizes. The deletion
requester's ideal answer is an \emph{empty} victim list -- so there is no
tool-independent list of vulnerable candidates for a predictor to
recover, and what our experiments test is the mapping from a chosen
objective to \emph{its own} damage distribution. Prediction
(Table~\ref{tab:channel-matrix}) asks how well each frozen ranking
anticipates each world's victims; protection (Table~\ref{tab:crossed})
acts on a ranking inside one world and re-measures the sealed audit.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:benchmark-protocol}

\paragraph{Objectives (channel-stratified roster).}
Output channel: GA, GradDiff, NPO, SimNPO, IdkDPO, GRU. Representation
channel: RMU, RepNoise, Circuit Breakers. All nine parents are configured
on development requests without access to susceptibility scores; their
objectives, trainable blocks, retain streams, learning rates, budgets, and
checkpoint grids are fixed before scoring. Damage is
Eq.~\eqref{eq:realized-damage} at the predeclared horizon, including
requests that fail the forget criterion.

\paragraph{Predictors.}
Gradient family: exact gradient norm (per-candidate backward; expensive
reference) and loss-shake energy, i.e.\ randomized sensitivity
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:loss-shake-identity}; $2R$ forward sweeps, ours).
Representation family: hidden-state proximity (matched probe),
sentence-embedding proximity, lexical overlap. Cautionary baselines:
forget-direction alignment (prespecified cautionary baseline;
Appendix~\ref{app:alignment-postmortem}), one random direction, initial
candidate NLL, answer length,
random ranking. Every predictor is frozen at $\theta_0$ and sealed before
any trajectory runs.

\paragraph{Datasets and models.}
The primary setting is TOFU single-author requests \cite{maini2024tofu}
with group-disjoint discovery/audit folds and a sealed native audit.
Replication settings re-run the reduced crossed roster
(\{GradDiff, NPO\}$\times$\{RMU, RepNoise\}) on WMDP-bio with an MMLU
retain universe \cite{li2024wmdp,hendrycks2021measuring} (the
representation family's native habitat), MUSE-News \cite{shi2024muse}
(real-world corpus, output-channel habitat), and RWKU \cite{jin2024rwku}
(pretrained knowledge with built-in neighbor audits). Stress settings
probe the honesty clauses: MUSE-Books (verbatim memorization) and PISTOL
\cite{qiu2024pistol} (maximal forget--retain structural entanglement,
where proximity probes are most at risk of over-selecting). Because each
dataset fixes the probes' base rates while the channel is fixed by the
objective, replication across these settings tests that the interaction --
not any single correlation level -- is the invariant. Mechanism runs use
Qwen2.5-1.5B in fp32, where the fidelity gate certifies the randomized
probe; Qwen2.5-7B in bf16 provides the cost table and scale transfer with
the exact-norm probe standing in where low precision fails the gate.

\paragraph{Protocol.}
Prediction metrics are within-request Spearman $\rho$, AUROC against the
realized top-$K_p$ damage set, and tail-restricted $\rho$; the primary
endpoint is the interaction of Eq.~\eqref{eq:interaction} with
candidate-level bootstrap intervals. Protection follows the crossed design
at a common preregistered forget-recall criterion with paraphrase audits;
outcomes are mean and $\operatorname{CVaR}_{.95}$ damage on the sealed
native audit, reach reported unconditionally. Uncertainty uses
hierarchical bootstrap over requests and seeds; leave-one-out ranges
accompany small top levels.

\subsection{Main Result I: Channel-Conditioned Prediction}
\label{sec:exp-prediction}

Table~\ref{tab:channel-matrix} reports Spearman $\rho$ between each sealed
predictor and realized damage for every objective, with objectives grouped
by declared channel and predictors grouped by family
(visualized in Figure~\ref{fig:channel-main}A--B). The reading is
the frozen family-level interaction $\Delta$, not uniform per-objective
dominance. Individual columns may be mixed or reversed; the per-family
channel means and bootstrap interval quantify whether the relative
gradient-versus-proximity advantage changes across declared channels.
Cross-channel signal and the cautionary alignment baseline are reported
rather than interpreted away. Flagged columns are retained rather than dropped:
$^{\dag}$ columns measure a weak-signal (near-static) regime and
$^{\ddag}$ marks criterion reach achieved only through model-wide
collapse, where per-candidate ranking measures fragility ordering within
an avalanche. Secondary metrics (AUROC, Overlap@$K_p$, tail $\rho$) for
the two headline probes appear in the appendix.

Unblinded, the family-level structure is exactly the predicted flip. The
gradient family's channel mean falls from $+0.38$ on output-channel
objectives to $-0.15$ on representation-channel objectives -- not merely
weaker but sign-reversed -- while the proximity family retains partial
signal on both sides ($0.32$ vs.\ $0.19$), the asymmetry declared in
Section~\ref{sec:setup-channels}. Both preregistered interaction pairs
exclude zero: GradDiff/RMU $\Delta=+0.300$ $[+0.131,+0.466]$ and
NPO/RepNoise $\Delta=+0.470$ $[+0.232,+0.698]$, and both replicate under
an independent seed ($+0.226$ $[+0.048,+0.406]$; $+0.737$
$[+0.532,+0.950]$; Appendix~\ref{app:seed-replication}). The cautionary
alignment baseline predicts nowhere ($|\rho|\le 0.11$ in every column),
and loss-shake energy tracks the exact gradient norm within $\pm 0.01$
in all nine columns -- the backward-free estimate loses essentially
nothing.

\subsection{Main Result II: Crossed Channel-Matched Protection}
\label{sec:exp-protection}

Prediction is operationally useful only if routing a budget through it
improves the sealed audit. Table~\ref{tab:crossed} reports the crossed
design over three parents (GradDiff for the output channel; RMU and
RepNoise for the representation channel): each parent runs unmodified to
its criterion-reaching checkpoint, then each selector arm spends an
identical repair budget under the identical guarded procedure of
Section~\ref{sec:method-protection} (Figure~\ref{fig:channel-main}C). Each
frozen score constructs its
protect pool and matched low-score reference stream; all other training
choices are held fixed. The preregistered success criterion is that the
matched arm's CVaR is below each of the mismatched, random, and none arms,
per parent. The
exact-norm selector is the expensive gradient-profile reference ceiling,
outside the criterion;
comparing it with randomized sensitivity prices the backward-free
discount. Any protection claim is conditional on comparable forgetting:
the reach and forget-recall columns expose attempts to buy retention by
under-forgetting, and a parent-floor regime is reported as unidentifiable
rather than as a selector win.

\textbf{Unblinded, the criterion is not met, and the pattern of its
failure is informative.} On GradDiff, the proximity (mismatched) pool is
by far the best (CVaR $2.97$ vs.\ $5.21$ matched and $5.35$ unprotected)
-- consistent with GradDiff's damage concentrating near the forget set,
where proximity also predicts it well ($\rho{=}0.49$). On RepNoise,
matched proximity beats both mismatched arms and no protection, but
\emph{random} allocation is lowest ($2.31$ vs.\ $2.49$) -- consistent
with RepNoise's damage being diffuse: no probe predicts it well (best
$\rho{=}0.14$), and a diverse pool covers a diffuse distribution better
than any concentrated one. RMU is a parent-floor regime (unprotected
CVaR $0.12$; no arm helps) and is reported as unidentifiable. Two
regularities survive across parents: randomized sensitivity tracks the
exact-norm ceiling wherever the gradient family is deployed (RepNoise
$2.63$ vs.\ $2.64$; GradDiff $5.21$ vs.\ $4.93$, both far from the
winning pool), so the backward-free discount carries over to protection;
and the guard holds forgetting at criterion in every arm. The coherent
reading is that \emph{repair transfer is governed by how well the
protect pool covers the realized damage distribution}: proximal damage
rewards proximity pools, diffuse damage rewards coverage, negligible
damage rewards abstention. Prediction is channel-conditional
(Table~\ref{tab:channel-matrix}); pool selection for repair should
follow the \emph{predicted damage distribution} rather than the channel
label alone. The $\alpha$ diagnostic (Section~\ref{sec:exp-alpha}) and
the low-proximity replications are the frozen instruments for that
refinement.

\subsection{Secondary Diagnostic: the \texorpdfstring{$\alpha$}{alpha} Sweep}
\label{sec:exp-alpha}

The confirmatory router uses only the declared endpoints: $\alpha=0$ for
output-channel parents and $1$ for representation-channel parents. We do
not estimate an interior deployment weight. Instead, after the primary
design was frozen, an append-only exploratory extension fixed the
nine-point prediction grid $\{0,0.125,\ldots,1\}$ and the protection subset
$\{0,0.25,0.5,0.75,1\}$. The endpoint arms in the latter are reused from
Table~\ref{tab:crossed}; only its three interior arms require additional
runs.

The prediction curve $\rho(s_\alpha,\mathrm{damage})$ is computed offline
from sealed scores and trajectories. Its per-objective maximizing
$\alpha$ is a descriptive readout of cross-channel signal, not a fitted
router and not a new endpoint. The protection curve analogously reports
sealed-audit CVaR for Top-$K_p(s_\alpha)$ under the unchanged guarded
budget. Its descriptive optimum need not match the prediction optimum,
because ranking individual risk and transferring repair from a selected
pool are different questions. We report all curves, including interior or
opposite-endpoint optima, without revising the fixed $0/1$ rule.
Unblinded, the prediction curves show a sharp, seed-stable asymmetry:
every output-channel objective peaks at an \emph{interior} weight and
beats both endpoints (GradDiff $\rho=0.61$ at $\alpha=0.5$ vs.\
$0.41$/$0.49$ at the endpoints; GRU $0.59$ at $0.5$; NPO and SimNPO
$0.47$/$0.46$ at $0.375$; replicated under seed 2026 with every
output-channel optimum in $[0.25,0.75]$), whereas every
representation-channel curve is monotone toward the $\alpha{=}1$ corner
(RMU $0.34$, RepNoise $0.14$, CB$^{\dag}$ $0.09$): the two nearly
uncorrelated probes are complementary where both carry signal and
anti-complementary where the gradient probe is blind. The interior
protection arms are pending. Full grids and plots are in
Appendix~\ref{app:alpha-diagnostic} (Figure~\ref{fig:alpha-sweep}).


\subsection{Backward-Free Fidelity and Cost}
\label{sec:exp-fidelity}

Appendix Table~\ref{tab:bwfree} tests the probe's two supporting claims. Fidelity is
the rank agreement between loss-shake energy and the exact gradient norm,
reported both inside and outside the gate; the cancellation, underflow, and
curvature regimes remain visible even if the gate fails to separate them.
At the gate-certified setting ($\eta{=}3\mathrm{e}{-}3$, $R{=}64$, fp32)
rank agreement is $0.94$ and the probe's damage prediction matches the
exact profile within $\pm 0.01$ on all nine objectives; in the
cancellation zone ($\eta{=}3\mathrm{e}{-}4$) agreement collapses to
$0.50$ and the gate withholds certification.
Cost: profiling an entire candidate universe takes $2R$ batched
forward sweeps at inference memory, versus per-candidate backward passes
at training memory; wall-clock and peak-memory measurements at 7B compare
the randomized probe against exact-norm, JVP, and vectorized
gradient-dot implementations (one symmetric pair sweep $5.97$\,s at
$18.3$\,GB, vs.\ $27.4$--$35.2$\,GB for forward-mode and vectorized
gradient machinery). The advantage we claim is memory and
backward-freedom, not universal wall-clock supremacy.

\subsection{Replication, Stress, and Positioning}
\label{sec:exp-supporting}

Replication runs repeat the reduced crossed roster on WMDP-bio, MUSE-News,
and RWKU; the confirmatory quantity in each is the interaction, not any
absolute correlation, since each dataset shifts the probes' base rates.
Stress runs target the honesty clauses: PISTOL maximizes forget--retain
entanglement (proximity probes risk over-selection), and MUSE-Books tests
verbatim-memorization removal. Seed and request replication on TOFU, the
specificity controls (benign SFT, unrelated requests, norm-matched random
motion), repair-budget sweeps, and the strong-method comparison that
places the full pipeline alongside recent unlearners under the same
contract are reported in the appendix. The pipeline is a layer over
parents, so that comparison is context, not the claim: the claim is the
pair of frozen prediction and protection interactions, reported directly.
